Mass TRAC Help

Data Overview

All of the arrival times are all calculated by the data provider (the MBTA and MassDOT), and Mass TRAC renders it in a more human-readable form. MassDOT, the MBTA, and the Mass TRAC authors are not responsible for any inaccuracies or issues that arise because of inaccuracies with the live data (read below for known bugs).

The data being displayed is quite simple. When you see a time listed next to a destination, it indicates how long you can expect to wait for a train going towards that destination to show up at the station you selected. Fresh data can be fetched manually by pressing the "Refresh" button.

Any service alerts found that affect the lines that stop at the station selected are also rendered and counted for your convenience. Additionally, if you click one of the trips, you can see all the associated predictions with it (how long it will take to run its route)!

The times in (parentheses)

Due to the smaller screens on mobile devices, certain shortcuts were taken to provide information in a clearer manner. The result of this is that the distinction between a predicted trip (one that's already running) and a scheduled trip (generally one that hasn't gotten to the end of the line and turned around yet) is denoted with the countdown times in parentheses.

Known Bugs

As we've developed Mass TRAC and tested data against it, we've come across some strange (and sometimes persistent) bugs that could affect the usefulness of the tool. Below are the biggest ones we've found.

  • Weekend Commuter Rail Data: The MBTA data feed seems to get polluted (mostly on weekends) with phantom weekday trips that will obviously never come by. That's why we print the Train # on Commuter Rail trips!
  • Loss of Tracking Data: On the Commuter Rail feeds, trips will sometimes lose their tracking data (which includes the real-time position and other information), which takes it from predicted mode to scheduled mode and back again. This reverts the times to whatever was printed on the schedule and thus won't (necessarily) reflect reality.
  • Feed Resilience: Things happen, sometimes the tracking data feeds disappear for a few minutes here and there. If one is encountered, Mass TRAC will tell you.
